Selection Methodology

Step-by-step logic for Trend Station Selection

1

Preparation & MPR Scoring

Every station is analyzed and assigned a Mean Parameter Ratio (MPR) score. This score quantifies the severity of contamination relative to permissible limits. Stations are then ranked dynamically within their respective districts based on this score.

Active Parameters for MPR:
Upload data to see active parameters...

The equation for the Mean Parameter Ratio (MPR) is:

MPR =
Σ n i=1
Pi Li
n

Where:

  • MPR = Mean Parameter Ratio
  • Pi = The value of the i-th parameter
  • Li = The permissible limit of the i-th parameter
  • n = Total number of parameters

Calculation Example:

  • Fluoride (P₁): 3.0 / Limit (L₁) 1.5 = 2.0
  • Nitrate (P₂): 22.5 / Limit (L₂) 45 = 0.5
  • Iron (P₃): 0.5 / Limit (L₃) 1.0 = 0.5
  • Numerator: Σ(Pᵢ/Lᵢ) (2.0 + 0.5 + 0.5) = 3.0
    Final MPR (3.0 ÷ 3 total params [n]) = 1.00
2

District Quota & Modes

A target quota is set per district (e.g., min 25%). If the District Low Sample Rule is enabled, districts with very few samples bypass the default rule and use a higher specific percentage.

Low Sample Rule Application:

If a district has 4 or fewer total samples, 100% of those samples are automatically added as trend monitoring stations to ensure sufficient statistical representation.

Example Scenario (Mode Application):

If a district has 100 background samples and 30 are contaminated, its Contamination Ratio is 0.30. This places it in the Moderate Groundwater Contamination District mode.

Therefore, when selecting the required trend monitoring stations for this district, the algorithm will ensure that 75% of the selected stations come from the pool of contaminated wells, while the remaining 25% are chosen from the fresh wells.

3

Phase 1: Contaminated Stations

If the quota allows, contaminated stations are selected first based on unique parameter failure combinations.

  • Step A: One representative station (highest MPR) is picked from each unique contaminant group.
  • Step B: If groups exceed the target quota, the groups with the highest severity (most failed params + highest MPR) are prioritized.
  • Step C: If groups are fewer than the quota, remaining slots are proportionally allocated back to the groups based on their size, pulling the next highest MPR candidates.

Example Scenario (Phase 1):

Requirement: Select 2 contaminated stations.
Available Groups:
• Group A (Fluoride & Nitrate fails) - 5 stations
• Group B (Iron fail only) - 3 stations
Action: The system picks 1 station from Group A (the one with highest MPR) and 1 station from Group B (the one with highest MPR) to precisely meet the quota of 2 while representing all contamination profiles.

4

Phase 2: Fresh Stations (50/20/30)

Remaining quota is filled using uncontaminated (fresh) stations following a strict 50/20/30 division:

  • 50% High MPR: Picks "At-Risk" fresh stations with the highest parameter ratios just below the limit.
  • 20% Low MPR: Picks highly pristine fresh stations as absolute baseline controls.
  • 30% Random Spatial: Randomly selects remaining fresh stations, enforcing the Spatial Radius Constraint to ensure geometric spread.

Example Scenario (Phase 2):

Requirement: Need 10 Fresh Stations to hit quota.
• High MPR (50% = 5 stations): The 5 fresh stations with MPRs closest to 1.0 (e.g., 0.92, 0.85...) are selected as they are "At Risk".
• Low MPR (20% = 2 stations): The 2 fresh stations with absolute lowest MPRs (e.g., 0.04, 0.08) are selected as baseline references.
• Random (30% = 3 stations): 3 stations are drawn from the remaining pool at random, only if they are over 5km away from the 7 stations just selected.

5

Spatial Exceptions & Fallbacks

Exempted Regions: Geographically small areas (e.g., Delhi, Lakshadweep, Puducherry, Chandigarh, Daman & Diu, Dadra & Nagar Haveli) automatically ignore the Spatial Radius rule so they don't fail to reach their quotas.

Fallbacks: If spatial constraints or a lack of fresh stations prevents the algorithm from reaching the target quota, the rules are gradually relaxed (e.g., dropping spatial requirements) or fulfilled using the next highest MPR contaminated stations to guarantee the District quota is met.
