System Methodology

How the Water Quality Index is Calculated

The WQI Formula (Brown et al., 1972)

Step 1 — Selection of Parameters

Choose relevant physico-chemical parameters (e.g., pH, TDS, Hardness, DO) for the spatial analysis.

Step 2 — Calculate Unit Weight (wi)

wi = k / Si | k = 1 / ∑(1 / Si)

Unit weights are inversely proportional to the recommended standards (Si). k is the proportionality constant.

Step 3 — Calculate Sub-Index (qi)

qi = 100 × |Va − Vi| / |Vs − Vi|

Calculates quality rating. Va = actual measured value, Vi = ideal value (0 for most, 7.0 for pH), Vs = standard limit.

Step 4 — Calculate Overall WQI

WQI = ∑(wi × qi)

Sum the products of unit weights and sub-indices. Note: Division by ∑wi is omitted because our algorithm normalizes weights so ∑wi = 1.

Sample Calculation Walkthrough

Below is the exact calculation performed for the first row of your uploaded data, analyzing all selected parameters dynamically.

1. Scenario Data

  • pH: Measured (Va) = 7.6
  • Ideal = 7.0, Standard = 8.5
  • TDS: Measured (Va) = 600
  • Ideal = 0, Standard = 2000

2. Calculate Weights (wi)

Find Constant (k)
∑(1/Si) = (1/8.5) + (1/2000)
= 0.1176 + 0.0005 = 0.1181
k = 1 / 0.1181 = 8.467
Apply Weights
wpH = 8.467 / 8.5 = 0.996
wTDS = 8.467 / 2000 = 0.004

3. Sub-Indices (qi)

pH Quality Rating
qpH = 100 × |7.6 - 7.0| / |8.5 - 7.0|
qpH = 100 × (0.6 / 1.5) = 40.0
TDS Quality Rating
qTDS = 100 × |600 - 0| / |2000 - 0|
qTDS = 100 × (600 / 2000) = 30.0

4. Final WQI

WQI = ∑(wi × qi)

(0.996 × 40.0) + (0.004 × 30.0)
= 39.84 + 0.12
39.96
Good Water
